📊 Peak Identification & Retention Times

HbA1c (Glycohemoglobin A1c) Retention time: 1.5 min | Diagnostic target | Lowest positive charge
Hb Variants (e.g., HbS, HbC, HbD, HbE) Retention time: 1.8-2.8 min | Flags variant interferences | Variable charge
HbA0 (Non-glycated Hemoglobin A) Retention time: 3.0 min | Baseline hemoglobin | Higher positive charge
HbA2 (Hemoglobin A2) Retention time: 5.0 min | β-thalassemia marker | Highest positive charge

🔬 How IE-HPLC Separation Works

Ion-Exchange Principle: Hemoglobin variants have different electrical charges. The gradient elution pushes components through a negatively-charged resin column:

  • Lowest charge (HbA1c): Elutes first (~1.5 min)
  • Intermediate charge (Variants): Elute in middle (~1.8-2.8 min)
  • Highest charge (HbA0, HbA2): Elute last (~3-5 min)

Detection: Dual-wavelength absorbance at 415 nm (primary) & 500 nm (background reference) for hemoglobin quantitation.

✓ Interpretation Guidelines

  • Normal Result: Three main peaks (HbA1c, HbA0, HbA2) with expected pattern. HbA1c% calculated from peak areas.
  • Variant Detected: Extra peak between HbA1c & HbA0 signals variant interference. System flags & reflexes to Variant Mode (60 T/H) for accurate HbA1c measurement.
  • CV ≤ 0.5%: Excellent repeatability within 21-day runs. Precision maintained even at 10,000 tests.
  • Clinical Decision Levels: HbA1c ≥6.5% = Diabetes diagnosis. Follow-up range: 5.7%-6.4%.
  • Reference Range (Normal): HbA1c 4.0%-5.6% (NGSP) or 20-38 mmol/mol (IFCC)
